Definition

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical "things"—devices, vehicles, or appliances—embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity to exchange data in real-time without human intervention.
It enhances efficiency, automation, and monitoring in areas like smart homes, industrial manufacturing, and city infrastructure, aiming to improve daily life.

Examples:

  • Smart Home: Smart lights, thermostats, and security cameras controllable via smartphones.
  • Industrial IoT: Machines in factories that detect needed maintenance.
  • Smart City: Sensors for traffic management, waste management, and environmental monitoring.
  • Healthcare: Wearable devices tracking vital signs in real time.
